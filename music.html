Music is a large part of my life, though I've never tried to make a living off it.

I'm a songwriter, play the bowed psaltery, and sing frequently even though I never got to be much good at it.

Every year, I participate in February Album Writing Month (FAWM), a challenge to write and record an album during the month of February. Most of these aren't currently available, as mixing and arranging is a much longer process than songwriting; but links to a few songs that are further along in that process or otherwise noteworthy can be found on this page.

Some projects I've worked on can be found below.

Across the Broken Land

Every year, I participate in February Album Writing Month, a challenge to write and record an album during the month of February. In 2018, I wrote eleven songs comprising the album I have titled Domains and Angles, which centers on themes of the difficulty of communication, the changing nature of the city in which I live, and non-euclidean geometry.

The full album is not currently available, as mixing and arranging is a much longer process than songwriting, but a sample song, The Season of Rust, can be found here.
